FAQs

A virus is a malicious program that can replicate itself and spread to other computers, often causing damage to files and systems. On the other hand, a trojan disguises itself as a legitimate program to trick you into downloading it.

Protection involves installing and regularly updating antivirus software, being cautious when downloading files or clicking on links, keeping all software up to date, and using strong passwords and two-factor authentication to protect your accounts.

It depends on how the files were deleted. If they were simply deleted from the recycle bin, they may be recoverable with file recovery software.

Phishing is a scam where attackers try to trick you into giving them sensitive information. To avoid it, double-check sender's email address, look for errors in the message, never click on unknown links or download attachments, and use strong passwords and two-factor authentication.
